    6. Internet commerce is great because it makes buying and selling goods and services easier, faster, and more convenient for both businesses and consumers. Through e-commerce, people can shop at any time from anywhere in the world, giving businesses access to a global market beyond physical boundaries. It reduces operating costs since companies do not need expensive retail spaces, while customers benefit from a wide variety of products, easy price comparisons, and secure digital payment options. Moreover, businesses can use data to personalize customer experiences and improve services, while innovations such as mobile money, online marketplaces, and digital delivery continue to transform how trade is done. Overall, internet commerce creates efficiency, accessibility, and growth opportunities that traditional commerce alone cannot provide.
    1. 1979 – The idea of electronic shopping was introduced by Michael Aldrich, who connected a TV to a computer through a telephone line.
    2. 1982 – Boston Computer Exchange launched as the first e-commerce company, specializing in selling used computers.
    3. 1991 – The internet became open for commercial use, making online trade possible.
    4. 1994 – Netscape introduced SSL encryption, which made secure online transactions possible. The same year, Pizza Hut offered online ordering, one of the first food e-commerce services.
    5. 1995 – Amazon and eBay were founded, becoming pioneers of modern e-commerce.
    6. Late 1990s–2000s – Online payments (like PayPal, launched in 1998) made e-commerce safer and more popular.
